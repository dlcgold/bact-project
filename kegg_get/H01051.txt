ENTRY       H01051                      Disease
NAME        Corynebacterium ulcerans infection
DESCRIPTION Human infections with toxigenic Corynebacterium ulcerans cause respiratory diphtheria-like illness. It can be fatal in unvaccinated patients and usually occur in adults, who consumed raw milk or had close contact with domestic animals. These C. ulcerans strains produce a diphtheria toxin, which is similar to that of C. diphtheriae, the causative agent of classical diphtheria.
