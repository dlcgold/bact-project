NAME        Scarlet fever;
            Scarlatina
  SUPERGRP  Streptococcal infection [DS:H00333]
DESCRIPTION Scarlet fever, also called scarlatina, is caused by Streptococcus pyogenes (GroupA Streptococcus, GAS). The important diagnostic signs of scarlet fever include fever, sore throat, rash and bright red tongue with a "strawberry" appearance. Scarlet fever can affect people of all ages, but it is usually attacking children under 10.
